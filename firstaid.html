Common Animal Health Issues

Quick access to information about frequent problems street animals face

Wounds & Injuries

Cuts, scrapes, bite wounds, and other injuries

Parasites

Ticks, fleas, mange, and worm infestations

Poisoning

Suspected poisoning or toxic substance ingestion

Respiratory Issues

Coughing, sneezing, difficulty breathing

Eye Problems

Discharge, swelling, cloudiness, or injury

Malnutrition

Extreme thinness, weakness, or starvation

Fractures

Broken bones or limping

Skin Conditions

Rashes, hair loss, itching, or infections

Safety First

Important precautions when helping street animals

Approach Carefully

Injured animals may be scared and defensive. Move slowly and speak softly.

Protect Yourself

Wear gloves and wash hands thoroughly after handling animals.

Traffic Awareness

Be mindful of traffic when helping animals on roads.

Know Your Limits

Provide only first aid you're comfortable with and seek professional help.
